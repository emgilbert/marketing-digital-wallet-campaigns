Learnings & Recommendations

Cross-experiment synthesis from Apple Pay R1, R2, and Google Pay.

What Works
✅

Incentives are essential for non-provisioned customers — messaging alone is insufficient to overcome the friction of first-time wallet setup.

✅

Simplified offers drive strong results — reducing from 5 to 3 taps maintained effectiveness while improving accessibility for lower-intent audiences.

✅

Non-provisioned is the highest-ROI target — Apple Pay R1 showed 2.7-month payback vs. 28 months for provisioned segment.

✅

Retention is durable — incentivized users maintained elevated Apple Pay usage 90–150 days post-campaign across both R1 and R2.

✅

$5 incentives are more cost-efficient; $10 drives more volume — clear efficiency vs. scale trade-off validated by Google Pay A/B test.

What Doesn't Work
❌

Incentivizing already-provisioned users — no statistically significant lift in Apple Pay or Google Pay usage vs. messaging alone in both experiments.

❌

Financial incentives for provisioned-but-inactive users — messaging alone achieves the same ~1.5% completion rate as $10 offers. Pure waste.

❌

Treating iOS and Android as equivalent audiences — Android baseline provisioning rates (~1.7%) are far lower than iOS (~5.7–6.1%). Requires different benchmarks and channel strategy.

Strategic Recommendations
1

Focus incentives on non-provisioned customers

Highest friction, highest ROI. Non-provisioned users have 2.7-month payback vs. 28 months for provisioned. Concentrate budget here exclusively.

2

Shift provisioned segment to message-only

Eliminate redundant incentive spend. Both Apple Pay and Google Pay data confirm messaging alone achieves equivalent results for already-provisioned users.

3

Integrate into always-on onboarding

Embed wallet incentives into early lifecycle journeys at the moment of highest intent — at or shortly after Card activation — rather than episodic campaigns.

4

Use $5 for efficiency, $10 for scale

$5 is ~2x more cost-efficient per provision. Use $10 only when growth targets require maximum absolute volume, with full awareness of the ROI trade-off.

5

Recalibrate benchmarks for Android vs. iOS

Android users have materially different baseline provisioning rates, channel engagement, and behavior patterns. Separate KPIs, channel mix, and incentive sizing are warranted.
